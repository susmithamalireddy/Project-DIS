Cities in Florida

Florida is home to many vibrant cities, each with its own unique character and attractions. From the bustling streets of Miami to the historical charm of St. Augustine, the Sunshine State offers a diverse array of urban experiences. Whether you're looking for the thrills of theme parks, the relaxation of beach towns, or the culture of art-centric locales, Florida's cities have something to offer for every type of traveler.

Average Monthly rent in cities of Florida

The below table shows the average rent prices for various cities in Florida for Q3 2022, indicating that Miami has the highest rent at $4,055, while Jacksonville is the most affordable at $1,858. Fort Myers and Fort Lauderdale are on the higher end, with rents above $3,000, whereas cities like Tampa, Orlando, and St. Petersburg fall in the mid-range around $2,200 to $2,657. The lower end features Tallahassee at $1,626 and Port St. Lucie at $1,953, offering more budget-friendly options.

In contrast, the second pie chart, "Land Area Distribution among Cities in Florida," shifts the focus to the spatial footprint of the cities. Here, Jacksonville stands out with an overwhelming 51.5% of the land area, underlining its expansive physical extent in comparison to its urban counterparts. The other cities possess markedly less land area, with Cape Coral at 7.9% and Tampa trailing behind, amongst others, which suggests that Jacksonville's land scale is significantly larger than what its population size would imply.
